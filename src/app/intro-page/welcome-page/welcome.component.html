How does the USA elect a president?

Every 4 years the USA holds a presidential election. The winner of the election is not the person who gets the most total votes, rather the person who get the most 'Electoral Votes'.

Each state is given a certain amount of Electoral Votes based (loosely) on their population. Which ever person gets the most votes in that state, gets all of their electoral votes.

This means a person who gets just 50.1% of the votes in Texas would get all 40 of their electoral votes, same as if they got 99% of the votes.

States with small population get 3 votes at a minimum, while large states can have 25, 50, or more!

Today, to become president, a candidate needs to receive 270 electoral votes out of the 538.

What do you do in this game?

You will be competing against just 1 opponent. The Republican Party will be represented by the color RED and the Democratic Party will be represented by the color BLUE.

Your job in this game is to win enough States & Electoral Votes to become president. You can raise money, spend it on advertisements, travel to states for campaign events, and weigh in on national issues to become more popular -- and win the election in states.

Every action you take will help you in a state. Focus on states that are worth lots of Electoral Votes, and that you have a shot of taking/protecting.

The Map

You will be given a map that shows how close the race is in each state.

Dark blue means the democrat is winning that state by a lot, while light blue means they are leading by only a few percentage points.

Dark red means the republican is ahead by a lot in that state, while pink means the republican is leading by only a small margin.

If a state is light yellow, that means the state is tied!

You can also use this map to see how many votes you could gain by winning that state, and see a bar chart of how many electoral votes fall under each color on the map.

Under the map, you will see how popular you are in a states shown as a Percentage. (A +3% means you are predicted to win that state by 3% of the vote. A state that is EVEN could vote for either candidate on election day.)

It is a good idea to check in on this often, and use it to decide which states you are going to target in your campaign.
