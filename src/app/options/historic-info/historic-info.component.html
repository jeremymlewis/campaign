Historic Election

You are playing with a historic map!

Election of 1876 background

The election of 1876 was a close race, in which Rutherford B Hayes won by only 1 electoral vote.

Electoral Vote distribution and state preferences were very different in 1876 than they are today. You will notice many states that were not yet added to the union.

This was the 2nd presidential election after the passing of 15th amendment in 1870 that gave black men the right to vote, but sadly many were still wrongly denied that right. Women were also not allowed to vote in this election.

Election of 1916 background

The election of 1916 was a close race, in which Woodrow Wilson won reelection.

Central issues of the election included: Entering World War 1, Segregation in the South, and Women's Suffrage.

Electoral Vote distribution and state preferences were very different in 1916 than they are today.

Alaska and Hawaii were not states yet in 1916! They became states in 1959.

Washington DC also had no electoral votes in 1916, the 23rd amendment was passed in 1961 to give them the power to vote for president.

Election of 1976 background

The election of 1976 was a close race, in which Jimmy Carter was elected president.

Gerald Ford lost the race, ending his presidency.

Central issues of the election included: The Watergate Scandal, International Affairs, and Economic Recession.
